# Aristotle Lecture Notes

A collection of lecture notes for lectures on the first volume of Aristotle's works in *The Great Books of the Western World*, presented in the summer of 2026.

Link to playlist: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=y3WczKPJlWE&list=PL0KmvrFqDNusD3pmn_vQK8hjRxY57O9eM&pp=sAgC

## About

This repository contains lecture notes prepared for a lecture series on Aristotle’s works as collected in the first volume of *The Great Books of the Western World*.

The notes are designed for live teaching, with an emphasis on clear exposition, whiteboard use, and guided discussion. They are not meant to replace Aristotle’s texts, but to help readers enter them with a better sense of context, structure, and philosophical purpose.

## Source Text

The lecture series follows the first volume of Aristotle’s works in *The Great Books of the Western World*. The volume includes Aristotle’s writings on logic, nature, metaphysics, psychology, and related natural-philosophical topics.

## Contents Covered

The lectures may include notes on:

- Aristotle’s life and intellectual background
- The works of the *Organon*
- The physical treatises
- *Metaphysics*
- *On the Soul*
- The shorter natural treatises, sometimes grouped as the *Parva Naturalia*

## Purpose

The aim of these notes is to make Aristotle teachable in a lecture setting. Each set of notes is intended to help organize the material around central questions, key distinctions, and memorable explanations.

The notes emphasize:

- The philosophical problem Aristotle is addressing
- The structure of the assigned text
- Important terms and distinctions
- Connections between Aristotle’s different works
- Ways to present the material clearly on a whiteboard

## Format

The notes are written primarily in LaTeX so that they can be copied, edited, compiled, and adapted for classroom use.

## Status

This repository is a work in progress. Notes may be revised, expanded, or reorganized as the lecture series develops.
